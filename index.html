About

Biography

Xueji You is an associate professor and master's supervisor at Shanghai University. His research focuses on interfacial energy conversion in nonferrous mineral and carbonaceous materials, low-energy pollution control and resource recovery, and the modeling and prediction of environmental chemical processes. His work combines mechanistic experiments, reactive transport modeling, and artificial intelligence to study mineral and carbonaceous material processes, redox-driven natural attenuation, and the performance of environmental remediation systems.

He has published more than 30 journal papers in journals including Environmental Science & Technology, Water Research, and Science of the Total Environment, and has filed more than ten patent applications. He has led or participated in projects supported by the National Natural Science Foundation of China, the National Key R&D Program of China, and municipal research programs, and was selected for the Shanghai Super Postdoctoral Incentive Program.

His representative contributions include green, low-energy remediation technologies based on naturally occurring iron minerals; integrated control strategies for pollution at seawater-freshwater interfaces; coupled physicochemical-microbial remediation models; and predictive frameworks jointly driven by mechanisms and data. His work has received a First Prize of the Shanghai Marine Science and Technology Innovation Award, and he has been recognized as an Outstanding Reviewer by Groundwater for Sustainable Development.
